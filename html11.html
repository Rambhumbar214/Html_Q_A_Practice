Why It Is Celebrated

Dussehra, also known as Vijaya Dashami, is a major Hindu festival that marks the victory of good over evil. It commemorates the triumph of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana, as depicted in the epic Ramayana. The festival signifies the importance of righteousness and virtue and serves as a reminder of the victory of truth and justice.

When It Is Celebrated

Dussehra is celebrated on the 10th day of the Hindu lunar month of Ashvin, which usually falls in September or October. The festival occurs at the end of Navaratri, a nine-night celebration dedicated to the goddess Durga.

What You Do on This Day

On Dussehra, people participate in various activities, including:

  • Reenactments of the Ramayana, known as Ramlila, where the story of Rama's victory over Ravana is dramatized.
  • Burning effigies of Ravana, Meghnath, and Kumbhkaran, symbolizing the defeat of evil forces.
  • Feasting on special foods and sweets.
  • Visiting temples and performing prayers to seek blessings for prosperity and happiness.
